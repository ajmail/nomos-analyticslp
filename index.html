01 — The Problem
A $500B blind spot

Legislators vote on billion-dollar appropriations with no way to ask: what happened to the last allocation? The data exists. Nobody built the connective tissue.

"Government makes billion-dollar decisions with less data than you'd use to buy a car."

The Federal Audit Clearinghouse holds years of financial statements. Grant databases exist. What's missing is an intelligence layer that synthesizes it into decisions — and holds intermediaries accountable for where the money goes.

Accountability gap
Current systems lack visibility at major intermediaries exceed founder disbursements — and no one's tracking it systematically.
Verification failure
Claims of "recycled capital" and impact go unverified in audits, presenting outcome metrics that lack standardized validation across different reporting frameworks.
Decision vacuum
Legislators have no scenario modeling before voting on nine-figure appropriations. They vote blind.
